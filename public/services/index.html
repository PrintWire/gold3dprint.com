Gold & Platinum Printing

Precious metal SLM printing bypasses lost-wax casting to produce jewelry directly from CAD — hollow forms, interlocking mechanisms, and filigree geometries that casting cannot achieve. 18K gold alloys (yellow, white, rose) in 15–25 µm particle size; 950 platinum; 999 Ag fine silver. All compositions are hallmark-compliant. Minimum feature: 0.3 mm wall thickness, 0.1 mm resolution on fine detail.

Custom Jewelry Production

Production jewelry via precious metal SLM: hollow pendants that reduce gold weight 50–70% while maintaining volume, interlocking ring-chain systems that print as assembled, pavé lattices for diamond setting in complex undercut geometries, and personalized items with unique identifiers printed directly. CAD-to-finished-piece in 5–7 days for one-of-a-kind work; 2–3 weeks for batch production.

Wax Pattern Replacement

For jewelers committed to lost-wax casting, 3D printed wax patterns (directly printable wax or castable resin) eliminate injection mold tooling. Resolution: 25–50 µm layer height on SLA/DLP wax printers — finer than most CNC-machined molds. Suitable for filigree, undercuts, and unique runs where wax injection tooling is uneconomical. Burn-out cycle compatible with standard investment.

Art & Luxury Objects

Precious metal printing for fine art and luxury: limited-edition sculptures in sterling silver, bronze, and 18K gold; architectural scale models in precious metal for luxury real estate marketing; bespoke functional objects (pen nibs, watch components, cufflinks). Parametric and generative design services available. All pieces documented with provenance certificate and material certification.
